About

This website pulls article headings and summaries from a variety of sources, such as the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and NPR News, and constructs sometimes reasonable and other times funny sounding article headings and summaries using markov chains.

Using BeautifulSoup4, Markov News scrapes the sources' websites and filters by heading and summary class. The code for this is located in this file

Markov chains are giant probability matrices. In the case of text, they create a square matrix of size however many unique words there are. Then, for each word in a row, they count how many times that word has followed the word of the row. To generate a chain, you randomly select a word based on how many times it has appeared after the current word you just generated.
